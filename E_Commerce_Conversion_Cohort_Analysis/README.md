# E-Commerce Conversion & Cohort Analysis

## Project Overview
This project analyzes user behavior across an e-commerce platform to understand how effectively users move through the purchase funnel and how long they remain active after their first purchase. The analysis focuses on conversion rates, retention patterns, and behavioral trends that influence long-term engagement and revenue.

---

## Project Structure
- **Raw Data:** Event-level logs capturing product views, cart opens, and purchases.
- **Conversion Funnel:**
  - Counted unique users at each stage (view, cart, purchase).
  - Calculated total conversion rate from first to final stage.
  - Calculated step-to-step conversion between funnel stages.
- **Cohort Analysis:**
  - Grouped users by first purchase month.
  - Calculated cohort age and month-over-month retention.
  - Compared retention patterns across cohorts.
- **Executive Summary:** Highlighted key friction points and retention trends.

---

## Key Insights
- The largest drop-off occurs between product views and cart opens, indicating mid-funnel friction.
- Overall conversion from view to purchase is about 10.3%, with a 35.6% conversion from cart to purchase.
- Retention is strongest in the first month after initial purchase and declines steadily over time.
- Cohorts show similar decay patterns, suggesting consistent user behavior across acquisition periods.
- Early re-engagement campaigns focused on the first month after purchase would have the highest impact.
