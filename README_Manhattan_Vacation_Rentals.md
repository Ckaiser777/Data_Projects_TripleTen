
Manhattan Vacation Rental Market Analysis
This project analyzes vacation rental performance across Manhattan neighborhoods to determine which areas and property types generate the strongest revenue. The goal of the project was to clean the data, prepare pivot‑table‑based analysis, and produce clear recommendations for investors based on revenue patterns.
Project Goal
The objective was to answer two key questions:
Which neighborhoods and property sizes (number of bedrooms) are most attractive for vacation rentals?
How much money did these listings generate?
The final deliverable includes an executive summary, supporting analysis, and recommendations for building a profitable short‑term rental portfolio.
---
The Data
The dataset included multiple fields describing listing performance, neighborhood, and revenue. Before analysis, several data‑quality issues needed to be addressed.
Key Fields Used
neighborhood — neighborhood name for each listing
bedrooms — number of bedrooms in the property
revenue_earned — total revenue generated by the listing
Additional fields used for grouping, cleaning, and standardization
Data Cleaning Steps
To prepare the dataset for accurate analysis:
Checked for and removed duplicate listings
Standardized neighborhood names (neighborhood_clean)
Normalized bedroom counts (bedroom_clean)
Created a top_listings column to identify high‑performing units
Re‑coded null bedroom values as 0 to represent studio units
Ensured consistent formatting for pivot tables and grouping logic
These steps ensured that revenue calculations and comparisons were accurate across neighborhoods and property types.
---
Assumptions
Revenue is based on the dataset’s revenue_earned field
All listings were assumed active and bookable during the dataset period
No adjustments were made for seasonality or occupancy fluctuations
Revenue does not include expenses (cleaning, maintenance, taxes, vacancy)
Neighborhood performance may reflect listing density as well as demand
---
Analysis Summary
The analysis revealed clear performance patterns across Manhattan:
Top neighborhoods: Midtown, Hell’s Kitchen, and the Lower East Side
Top property sizes: 1‑bedroom and 2‑bedroom units
Total revenue:
1‑bedroom units: $7.0M
2‑bedroom units: $4.46M
Studios: $2.2M
Top listing: A 2‑bedroom in East Harlem generating $208,980 annually
Top 10 listings average: $222,958.80 annually
Maximum potential annual revenue following recommended strategy: $359,280
---
Results
The strongest opportunities for investors are concentrated in Midtown, Hell’s Kitchen, and the Lower East Side. Across all neighborhoods, 1‑ and 2‑bedroom units consistently deliver the highest and most reliable revenue.
Larger units show high earnings per listing but appear too infrequently to support a scalable investment strategy.
Recommendation
Investors should prioritize 1‑ and 2‑bedroom units in high‑demand neighborhoods. This approach offers the best balance of revenue potential, availability, and long‑term scalability.
